"That feature alone could be a hit" - The Development Story of Photo Editing App "Fuchidori!" Born from a Single Twitter Comment

The story of how an app emerged from the "mono" SNS concept

Today, I released a new iOS app called "Fuchidori!"

This app allows you to easily cut out people and objects from photos with just one finger, add borders of your desired color and thickness, and instantly create stylish sticker-like images. It's a simple and fun app.

However, behind the birth of this app lies another SNS concept I had been working on and a "fateful comment" from someone. Today, I'd like to share the behind-the-scenes story of this development.

It All Started with the "mono" SNS Concept

Originally, I was conceptualizing an SNS called "mono" - a platform for recording and sharing the value of your beloved "things."

If marketplace apps are places to "sell things," then "mono" would be a place to "cherish things." That was the world I was aiming to create. As an important feature of this SNS, I was developing a "background removal & border feature" to make photos of posted items more attractive.

Because people love their items, I wanted them to post them in the best possible way. That was the passion I put into creating this feature.

The Tweet That Changed Everything

One day, I casually shared a demo video of this "border feature" on Twitter (X).

Then, hirothings (@hirothings) left this comment on that tweet:

"Amazing, that feature alone could be a hit"

This comment was truly a bolt from the blue.

I had been so focused on creating the large SNS called "mono" that I couldn't see the brilliance of "that one feature" that might be the most valuable part.

What users really want might not be complex apps packed with many features, but simple, powerful tools that solve one problem exceptionally well.

Encouraged by hirothings' words, I made a decision:
"Alright, I'll create the world's most user-friendly app focused solely on this feature!"

And Thus "Fuchidori!" Was Born

"Fuchidori!" was born from this background. What I focused on was making it simple, fun, and secure above all.

  • Surprisingly simple operation that anyone can use intuitively.
  • Free choice of colors and thickness to create your original works.
  • And the biggest commitment: complete offline operation that absolutely protects your privacy. Your precious photos will never be sent to any server.

Whether you use it to make marketplace product photos stand out, express your personality in SNS profile pictures, or enjoy "fan activities" with photos of your favorite idols - the usage is up to you.

Finally

I'd like to take this opportunity to express my heartfelt gratitude to hirothings, who provided the major catalyst for this development. Thank you so much.

A small feature has taken flight as a standalone app, triggered by one warm comment.
If you'd like, please download it and share your "Fuchidori" creations on social media! Nothing would make me happier than if this app could add even a little color to your daily life.
